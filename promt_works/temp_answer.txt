Yes, India is a democratic country — and it's the world's largest democracy by population.

India became a democratic republic on January 26, 1950, when its Constitution came into effect. Key features of Indian democracy include:

- **Parliamentary system** with a President as head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government
- **Universal adult suffrage** — every citizen over 18 (originally 21, lowered to 18 in 1988) can vote
- **Multi-party elections** held regularly at national, state, and local levels
- **Independent judiciary** with the Supreme Court at its apex
- **Federal structure** that divides power between the central government and states

With over 900 million eligible voters, India's elections are among the largest democratic exercises in the world. The Election Commission of India oversees the process.
